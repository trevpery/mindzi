The Learning Journey

The learning platform is where students prepare. It opens with a Foundation module that explains how AI actually works, then moves through six dimensions - from giving AI clear instructions, to spotting when it's wrong, to using it creatively and responsibly. Each one is self-paced and built around real situations rather than definitions, guided by AIRA, an AI character who deliberately makes mistakes so students learn to catch them. Most students complete it over a few sittings.

Foundation and dimensions

Students begin with a Foundation module, then move through six future-readiness dimensions: prompting clearly, spotting errors, using AI creatively, and applying judgment in real situations.

Foundation - Before You Begin

Before any dimension unlocks, every student builds the mental model the rest depends on: what AI actually is, how it learns from data, and why it gets things wrong. It is mandatory, it is assessed, and it is the reason the six dimensions make sense.

Dimension 01
Prompt Engineering
Can the student communicate precisely with AI to get the best output? The sharper the instruction, the more useful the result - and this skill transfers to every job.
A journalist prompting AI to research a breaking story under deadline pressure.
Dimension 02
Spotting Hallucinations & Bias
Can the student tell when AI is wrong, misleading, or systematically unfair? Confidence in an AI's output is not the same as accuracy, and the difference matters enormously.
A doctor questioning an AI diagnostic before it becomes a treatment decision.
Dimension 03
AI in the Real World
Does the student see how AI is already reshaping industries and jobs - and which type is changing their chosen field right now?
A farmer using AI for crop prediction, pest detection, and market timing.
Dimension 04
Creative Use of AI
Can the student use AI as a genuine creative partner, not just a shortcut? The vision, the cultural knowledge, and the judgment must stay human.
A designer collaborating with generative AI on a culturally authentic campaign.
Dimension 05
AI Safety & Ethics
Does the student understand responsibility, privacy, and the limits of ethical AI use? When AI causes harm, someone must be accountable - and knowing where responsibility lies matters in every field.
A lawyer deciding whether to trust AI-generated evidence before presenting it in court.
Dimension 06
AI & The Future
AI is moving fast, and this is where it's heading: agentic AI that takes actions on its own, RAG systems that ground their answers in real sources instead of guessing, and the shift coming to every profession. Can the student picture the landscape beyond today's chatbots - and judge any new AI system they meet?
A policymaker shaping the regulations that govern increasingly powerful AI systems.

The Ranked Certificate
More than a score. A specific record of how your child thinks.
Every student receives a personalised digital certificate. Rather than a generic "participant," it records their national percentile, their score across all six dimensions, and the one dimension where they were strongest - for example, "Top 8% nationally, strongest in spotting AI errors." A percentile shows where a student stands among everyone who competed: top 8% means they scored higher than 92 out of every 100 students nationally.

Goes beyond recall
Knowing the definition of bias is not the same as catching it in a live scenario. Mindzi is built around the second kind of thinking - applying ideas under pressure: questioning a biased output, deciding what to do with a risky one, directing AI precisely.
Applied judgment, not technical knowledge
A student can learn what AI is and still not know what to do with it. Should they trust the answer? Question it? Ask a better question? Look for evidence? Mindzi focuses on those decisions - the judgment that turns knowledge into action.
A national stage to apply it
Schools help students learn. Learning becomes meaningful when it is applied. MINDZI gives them a chance to apply those skills in a national challenge alongside their peers. Through rankings, leaderboards, and personalised performance insights, students see how their future-ready thinking compares at a national level.
